Welcome!

Self-proclaimed space cowgirl, I am a Galactic archeologist investigating the origins of the Milky Way at the University of Texas at Austin advised by Dr. Keith Hawkins. I research galaxy evolution by using stellar spectroscopy to analyze the chemical abundances of stars in our Galaxy, an endeavor that started when I obtained my B.S. in Astrophysics from the University of Florida.

Research

During my first semester of graduate school, I researched Massive Alpha Enhanced (MAE) stars in the APOGEE dataset. These stars' masses would imply that they are young, yet their [alpha/Fe] abundances suggest they are older. Along with Dr. Keith Hawkins and Dr. Zachary Maas, I calculated their 12C/13C ratios to see if they provide any hints towards formation pathways. This project is currently ongoing.

I am presently working on uncovering chemical azimuthal variations in the Galactic disk. Using APOGEE DR-17, I find a negative radial [Fe/H] gradient in the disk of -0.0702 dex/kpc. After modeling this gradient, I find azimuthal variations in [Fe/H], as well as other elements such as [O/Fe], [Mg/Fe], and [Mn/Fe]. Separating our sample by age and coupling these results with the [X/Fe] variations help us narrow down what processes drive Galactic evolution.

After wrapping up these projects, I look forward to using the stars incidentally observed during the Hobby-Eberly Telescope Dark Energy Experiment (HETDEX) to identify more metal-poor stars in the halo and create an unbiased Metallicity Distribution Function of the Milky Way.

Publications

Linked below are projects that I conducted with amazing colleagues in the realms of stellar and Galactic astrophysics.

Novel Uranium Lines

Shah et al 2023
New U II lines at 4050 and 4090 Å prove to be reliable and render abundances in agreement with the canonical 3859 Å line enabling robust calculations of nucleocosmochronometric ages.

Ultra Li-rich [Fe/H]-poor Red Giant

Kowkabany et al 2022
Discovery of a metal-poor ([Fe/H]=-2.43), r-process enhanced ([Eu/Fe]=+1.34) Milky Way Halo red giant with an ultra high Li abundance of A(Li)(3D,NLTE)=5.62 and [Li/Fe]=+7.00.
